What do you currently do?
I design and prototype next generation mobile search experiences at Yahoo.
Recently I have co-facilitated the end-to-end product design for a hypothesis called Hive Q&A, where it aims to cultivate communities around questions and quick answers for students and young professionals. Hive has been freshly released to iOS and Android app stores in Canada and the United States.
Currently, I'm digging into the nascent world of search bots, specifically designing easy bot creation at scale.
Why do you design?
I delight in giving users their daily smile or empowering them to knock out daily goals. Ultimately, good design augments our ability to architect meaningful lifestyles.
Academically trained as an engineer, I enjoy digging into the mechanics of my design concepts: I like toggling between big picture and fine details when crafting products. With my diverse skills, I have led design efforts for a wide array of problem domains: mobile search, editorial design, publishing, digital advertising, and infrastructural engineering. I've been in the UX field for 7+ years.
What inspires your creativity outside of UX?
I am inspired by various media, whether it's visual arts, music, or cooking. Especially in the past couple of years in the UX field, cooking—specifically modernist cuisine—has been a metaphor for experience prototyping for me.
While I find pleasure in hacking with my sous vide machine or learning more about different food form factors and textures caused by interesting scientific phenomena, I also thrive from conceptualizing a holistic eating experience. The best experiences, in my opinion, require a balance of intuition vs trial and error.
What was your experience prior to Yahoo?
I graduated from California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo in 2009 with a BS in Computer Science, and completed software engineering internships at Apple and Hewlett-Packard.
