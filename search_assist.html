Supercharging the Search
Autocompletion Experience

Prototyping, Interaction Design

Introduction

The team wanted to experiment with making Yahoo Search Assist (search autocomplete) even smarter. Given a user's need for immediacy when it comes to searching, we explored exposing more of our Knowledge Graph within the Search Assist module. The following prototype proposes the surfacing of “quick facts” (e.g. current times in different locations, measurement conversions) and relevant news articles that closely correlate to an entity.


Exposing the Knowledge Graph

Yahoo stores plenty of information about entities for different domains. For each item in the autocomplete module, we explored various visualizations of attributes of our Knowledge Graph.



News Discovery

One of the concepts we played around with is the idea of surfacing a relevant article to aid in discovering fresh news (e.g. for Steph Curry).

To simulate this interaction, I utilized jQuery's autocomplete and created a news article template. I then appended relevant images from our mocks at the bottom of the module given a query.



Quick Answers

For each substring of a suggestion, I pushed a new batch of mock data to the Search Assist module. These are raw strings that I eventually decorate with custom CSS (e.g. type treatments, spacing).

We believed that this snappy autocomplete experience could work well with highly utilitarian use cases (e.g. discovering time, unit conversions).

Lessons Learned

  1. I was re-humbled by the fact that there is a fine balance to be struck when it comes to providing the exact answer vs something that is purely meant for discovery.
  2. To create this snappy experience, I unminified jQuery's autocomplete module. I was able to adapt to insert my changes and quickly validate our hypotheses in lab. The adage "Good Artists Copy; Great Artists Steal" proved fruitful.
