Yahoo Search Assist:
Quick Answers and Enhancements

Prototyping, Interaction Design

Introduction

The team wanted to experiment with making Yahoo Search Assist (search autocomplete) even smarter. Given a user's need for immediacy when it comes to searching, we explored exposing more of our Knowledge Graph within the Search Assist module. The following prototype proposes the surfacing of “quick facts” (e.g. current times in different locations, measurement conversions) and relevant news articles that closely correlate to an entity.


An Enriched Experience

To create this snappy experience, I utilized jQuery's autocomplete module and inserted my own templates to decorate all of the suggestion batches we wanted to test in Lab.

For each substring of a suggestion, I pushed a new batch of data to the Search Assist module. These are raw strings that I eventually decorate with custom images, spacing, or appended modules.

Depending on the intent, we render a type of experience via custom HTML. I utilized both CSS and image slivers of our mocks to mimic the aesthetic.

Lessons Learned

  1. I was re-humbled by the fact that there is a fine balance to be struck when it comes to providing the exact answer vs something that is purely meant for discovery.
  2. jQuery's autocomplete module was a joy to deconstruct. After un-minifying it, I was able to quickly adapt to insert my changes. The adage "Good Artists Copy; Great Artists Steal" proved fruitful.
