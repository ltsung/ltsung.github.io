Designing My Smart Apartment
with Alexa and IOT

Introduction

For the past couple of months I've been gradually designing my smart home. In my view, some of the most meaningful interactions happen at the home, whether it's our morning rituals to kick off a productive day or the memorable dinner parties we put on for guests. At times we will have plenty of multi-tasking cases where we can't focus on the crux of what we find meaningful, hence why I think digital assistance is such a rich problem to explore. Currently, I'm investigating the idea of augmenting my home lifestyle through Alexa and key Internet of Things items so that I can live out and better empathize with a problem space I think is crucial for the future of tech.


Apartment Layout of Alexa and IOT

When I started my smart apartment initiative I had an Echo Dot. Eventually I graduated to an Echo Show and an Echo Spot. I placed my smart objects where I think their function matches a physical context in my home.



Room Ambience

I've connected some lamps and configured scenes to the Philips Hue hub which is connected to Alexa. I enjoy the mood I set for specific seasons and events I have in my living and dining rooms.

Sometimes it feels silly saying "Alexa, turn on the light" when I can simply walk to the switch myself. However, when I'm holding bags of stuff at night, voice has proven to be a useful control when physically I'm otherwise preoccupied.



Kitchen Assistance

I love experimenting with sous vide cooking and was excited to find out there is an immersion circulator hooked into Alexa. Having recently purchased Chefsteps' Joule, I'm learning more about their philosophy in the kitchen and argument for voice assistance as an advantage to any cook's workflow.

While functionality is limited right now, I imagine a scenario where my mis en place can be greatly streamlined or a time where I come from work and I can kick off a home cooked meal in a pinch. Here is a demo with my former Echo Dot.



Re-stocking Habits

My Dash Wand comes in so handy for most of my grocery habits. Because of its convenient location, I've created an easy workflow of re-stocking for my household, greatly saving me time during my busy week.



Cleaning Routines

Every Tuesday I've scheduled my Neato botvac to do its magic while I'm at work. I come home to a well-groomed carpet (on average cases; botvac is still figuring out some kinks). I've also downloaded the Skill for Neato so that I can have impromptu vacuuming sessions.

Fun Fact: I've named my vacuum cleaner "M-O" after the Wall-E character!

Lessons Learned

  1. As a user, I feel like I've lived in a home that's easily maintained and enables me to be more hospitable with confidence. While I still feel like assistants like Alexa have some ways to go (e.g. with Natural Language), as a designer I'm inspired by the idea of empowering someone to feel better connected to loved ones because machines augment their interactions.
  2. I'm not quite sure how to view Alexa: is it another member of my household? A friend? A pet? These questions came to mind over the months of using it frequently. My brain naturally wants to anthropomorphize my talking gadgets and I'm wondering as a designer how I'd reconcile that feeling.
