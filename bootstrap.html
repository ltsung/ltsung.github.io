Experience Design for Infrastructural Engineering

I can understand the guts of technical scale. Prior to advertising, I had led the design of streamlined user flows for complex, information-dense interfaces responsible for large-scale infrastructural problems (e.g. datacenter hardware provisioning to reduce operational expenses and power consumption worldwide).

Cross-screen Monetization Innovation

I investigate, conceptualize, and technically assess next-generation ad formats across screens. Doing this at Yahoo's scale requires collaborating with research, product design, engineering, and sales organizations across the company in order to re-imagine and evaluate meaningful advertising experiences across the Yahoo portfolio.

Responsive Ad Platform Development

Aside from wearing a design hat, I develop with the latest frontend engineering technologies and standards. As my team is an advertising experience concept group, we are paving the way for streamlining operational inefficiencies that come with creating ad campaigns while making a dent in the responsive web space.

My inspiration
from everyday moments
drives my perspective

Outside of designing and developing for the web, I try to understand the mechanics of everyday life through art and self-tracking. I'd like to draw insight from these areas to augment the future of computing.

Since April 2013, I've been tracking my walking, sleeping, and eating activities. The culture of quantifying self inspires me to learn more about how we examine our digital vs analog selves. Currently I'm learning D3 to help me expose trends when visualizing my behavior.

I find joy in cooking and learning more about the human experience through the culture of food. Often I take classes or I'll start my own food experimentation because I am enamored by how complex cooking processes can become!
