Auto-making Alexa Skills with
Google Spreadsheets and Node

Prototyping

Introduction

As a prototyper interested in rapid experimentation, I'm always on the lookout for automating tasks. I saw an opportunity to utilize the Google Spreadsheets API to parse high volumes of utterances and expected responses to generate multiple barebones Alexa Skills in parallel. I use Node.js to output the interaction model and AWS Lambda functions needed for Skill training. In this case study, I'll be demonstrating an early version of my homemade tool. This was the cornerstone to my design and prototyping efforts for the Crafting an NBA Q&A Experience through Alexa project.


New Prototyping Methodology

I wanted to create a workflow that enabled the entire team to brainstorm and design answers in a common tool: Google Spreadsheets seemed to be the best for parallel editing and visualizing text comparisons. I also wanted to create an automation that stayed true to what is supported by the Alexa SDK in the case of needing to evolve the Skill prototypes themselves.

My script will parse a Google Spreadsheet and map one utterance (e.g. a user's question) with N possible answer variations. Each variation column is eventually translated into an Alexa Skill, where the intent, utterance, and answer rows represent a possible "turn".

Using Alexa SDK and AWS

In under 5 minutes I can build new batches of Skills! The work for the developer (and fellow stakeholders) is more in the planning and designing of the interactions!



Testing on Echosim

If I'm not testing on my Echo device, I'm using Echosim.io for simulation. After hooking up my Amazon account, I can easily access my Alexa Skill locally even if I haven't deployed to the Alexa Skill Store.

Lessons Learned

  1. This was a super effective method of rapid prototyping for basic VUI. It allowed the team to test all of our flavors of utterances with little effort, compared to the manual and more cumbersome prototyping tools currently in existence.
  2. Given that my team of knowledge engineers have yet to delve into Alexa Skill making, I was able to provide early insight about the Alexa NLU and the technology suite in general. Essentially, it kept us grounded with what's truly feasible.
  3. I imagine that this is the beginning of streamlining workflows and enabling powerful expression for a designer. This could be a helpful tool for non-programmers to map out their Skill design in Google Spreadsheets and interact with developers with more ease.
