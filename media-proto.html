Project Overview

When I worked in the Media organization within Yahoo, I facilitated prototyping efforts for a variety of projects. Whether it was the Yahoo homepage on desktop or a fresh mobile concept demonstrating a radical way of consuming the news, I played dual role of designer and developer in order to quickly shed light on design possibilities. I primarily use web frontend technologies in a throwaway context in order to mimic nuanced interactions across devices. In addition, I took on the responsibility to partner closely with User Experience Research teams to craft user testing scripts and observation reports for the team.

Yahoo Homepage

I supported other designers through prototyping various responsive homepage layouts, navigation systems that interface across the Yahoo network, animations and page interactions. This prototype tests an interaction around displaying reading progress to a user.

Tech Used:  HTML/CSS3, Bootstrap (Responsive Grids), SASS, Handlebars, jQuery, Gulp/Grunt (Automation), Yeoman.io (Scaffolding)

Immersive Article Reading

The design team wanted to explore new ways of immersing into rich article experiences. Here are two prototypes around the Apple Watch that showcase playful animations and interactions upon vertical scroll. The goal was to enable a user to feel literally in tune with the mechanics of the Apple Watch while consuming the article.

Tech Used:  HTML/CSS3, jQuery, Gulp/Grunt, Yeoman.io

Interactive Ad Formats

In addition to traditional IAB standard ad formats and native ads, we wanted to explore new interactions to increase visibility and engagement. Here I partnered with another designer to create this parallax-based ad.

Tech Used:  HTML/CSS3, jQuery, Gulp/Grunt, Yeoman.io

Communities Around Content

The Media organization wanted to experiment with new ways of constructing communities around content within the Yahoo network. Here is an early mobile prototype I took to lab to validate key value propositions. This eventually formed the framework for the widely released Yahoo app, also known as Yahoo Newsroom.

Tech Used:  FramerJS, Frameless
